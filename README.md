# Cs-250-Portfolio
This repository includes my Sprint Review and Retrospective from the SNHU Travel project. In this project, I worked through different Scrum roles and applied Agile practices like user stories, sprint planning, testing, and retrospectives. This artifact shows how I used Agile methods to complete a project and reflect on what I learned.

Reflection
How do I interpret user needs and implement them into a program? How does creating “user stories” help with this?

I interpret user needs by breaking them down into smaller, clear requirements that can be built step by step. Creating user stories helps a lot because they focus on what the user wants and why it matters. Instead of just building features, I think about the user’s experience. User stories also include acceptance criteria, which helps make sure the feature works correctly.

How do I approach developing programs? What Agile processes do I hope to incorporate into my future development work?

I approach developing programs by breaking work into smaller pieces and focusing on one task at a time. Agile helped me understand the importance of working in steps instead of trying to do everything at once. In the future, I want to keep using Agile practices like sprint planning, daily standups, and continuous testing. These help keep projects organized and make it easier to fix problems early.

What does it mean to be a good team member in software development?

Being a good team member means communicating clearly, helping others when needed, and staying responsible for your work. It also means being open to feedback and willing to adjust when things change. In Agile, teamwork is very important because everyone depends on each other to complete the project. A good team member supports the group and helps the team succeed.
